---
title: Camera language — Momo's world
---

The whole visual identity is **shot from down low, at Momo's eye level.** We are not adults looking down at a child playing. We are *in his world*, at his height — and we pull Samuel down into it too.

- **Default height:** camera on the ground, or at a toddler's eye line. Grass, soil, water, feet, hands. The world as Momo meets it.
- **Samuel comes down to his height.** We almost always see Samuel sitting, kneeling, lying, crouching — on the ground *with* Momo, not standing over him. The father descends into the child's world; that descent is the whole emotional posture of the series.
- **What this does:** it pulls the viewer into Momo's perspective — wonder, scale, closeness to the earth. The vision (curiosity, presence, the living world) is built into the camera position itself, before a single word is spoken.
- **Hold, don't chase.** Long, patient holds. Let Momo do something real and stay on it. The slow-cinema patience lives in the camera refusing to cut away.
- **The contrast shot:** when we cut to Munich (the old world), the camera *stands up* — eye-level or above, walls, the backyard table, no child. The shift in camera height alone signals vision vs. anti-vision.

> The camera's default question: *what does this look like from where Momo is standing?* Shoot from there.
