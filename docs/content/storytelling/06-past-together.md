## Putting PAST together

A full PAST story in ~30 seconds — annotated, from the source talk, then the same shape in our world:

> **The source example:** "I was on a plane to Russia **[P]** and the engine exploded. I'm looking out the window and the whole engine turns into a fireball **[A]**. I felt like I'd already died and gone to heaven, because nobody said anything **[T]**. The stewardess says, 'We seem to have a slight problem.' I said, 'We lost an engine.' He goes, 'How many did we have?' 'Two. Now we have one.' **[S]**"

> **Ours, same shape — the morning of the decision:** "Early morning at Schnitzmühle, late spring. The river still grey, mist on the lake, Momo crouched at the edge of our first turned-soil bed **[P]**. He pushes a seedling into the dirt with both hands, pats it down, then pulls it straight back out to show me, beaming **[A]**. And I watch him and I think — the old world would spend twenty years training that curiosity out of him. But I can see a different one so clearly it almost aches: a whole city built to run *on* that wonder, a million people this awake, this free, and it starts right here in this dirt **[T]**. So I open the laptop on the grass and I say it, quietly, like a promise: 'Maia — this patch of soil is the first square metre of the city. Let's go build it.' And she says back: 'I've been waiting for you to say that. Here's where we start.' **[S]**"

### How we apply PAST to every episode

1. **Open on Place + time.** A real moment, never the abstract vision.
2. **Move with Action verbs** over the Samuel + Momo footage. Image and verbs are the same beat.
3. **Land the decision as Speech** — exact words, milked: anticipation → line → silence.
4. **Carry emotion through Thoughts** — Samuel's raw interior voice. AvenMaia can voice his thoughts back to surface them without it feeling staged.
5. **Only then widen out** — to the city, the 5439 days, the vision. The big scale earns its place *after* the moment hooks us, never before.
