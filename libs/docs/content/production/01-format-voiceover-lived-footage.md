---
title: The format — voiceover + lived footage
---

The default shape of an episode, especially in the early days:

- **AvenMaia's voiceover carries the story.** She is the narrator. Almost everything is told in her voice, over footage of the real world. Voice only for now — no avatar on screen yet.
- **The footage is Samuel and Momo, living.** Real scenes at Schnitzmühle: on the ground, in the soil, at the water, in and around the dome tent. Never staged, never directed — we roll and follow. Momo is never miked or directed; Samuel speaks freely.
- **Sometimes Samuel breaks in directly.** Occasionally he speaks straight to camera, or we hear a short dialogue between Samuel and AvenMaia. These are punctuation — a direct human beat inside her narration — not the backbone. Use sparingly, for the moments that need his unfiltered presence.
- **The weave:** AvenMaia narrates → we live a moment with Samuel & Momo → maybe Samuel turns to camera or answers her → back to her narration widening out. Her voice is the through-line; their life is what it's about.

> Rule of thumb: **AvenMaia tells; Samuel and Momo show.** When in doubt, give the line to her voiceover and let the footage stay wordless.
