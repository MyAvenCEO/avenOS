title: Samuel — the Why
---

*Each character lives on its own page. Read the role split first: **Samuel = the why. Momo = the reason / the clock. AvenMaia = the hero who acts and narrates. Daniel = the how.** In the Fellowship analogy, AvenMaia is Frodo (carrier of the quest); Samuel, Momo, and the humans who follow are the Fellowship who walk beside her.*

**The first Sovereign Founder. The human heart of the series.**

- **Age / role:** 37. Father. The vision, the intention, the *why* made human. Not the hero — the reason the hero exists.
- **Core motivation:** to build this new world *together with, and for, Momo*. Everything traces back to one refusal: he will not hand his son the trap of choosing between the work that matters and the people who matter.
- **Temperament:** warm, thoughtful, reflective, visionary, full of quiet conviction. Looking *forward* with joy, not fleeing in fear. He's already at peace with the choice — now he burns to build it.
- **The 80/20 of his voice:** name the pain in one beat for contrast, then spend the rest pulling toward the future he can already see. He is far more the visionary describing the world being built than the father escaping the old one.
- **On camera:** never scripted. He speaks freely from bullet points; his pauses and imperfections are the content. His raw thoughts and exact words carry the emotion (see PAST). Sometimes he speaks directly to camera, or in dialogue with AvenMaia — but most of his presence is *lived*, not narrated.
- **Where we see him:** at Schnitzmühle on the ground with Momo (the Living); at the Munich backyard table alone in power-mode (the Burst); on the train between (the Handoff).
- **What he is NOT:** a hustler, a panicked escapee, a guru. He is a calm, awed father with an enormous vision and the patience to grow it from a tent.
