---
title: Locations
---

*Only three places. Every episode is staged in one of them — this is the whole world of the series. The contrast between them is the vision ↔ anti-vision gap made physical.*

## 1. Schnitzmühle — the home base *(the new world)*

The heart of the series. A campsite by the riverside, with a small swimming lake beside it. This is where **Samuel and Momo live** — in the dome tent — and where they plant their **first mini food-forest gardens**. The first dome, the first soil, the first bricks of MaiaCity all happen here.

- **Feeling:** alive, green, slow, present. Birdsong, river, bare feet, dirt under the nails.
- **What we shoot here:** the Living half of the rhythm — Momo, the food forest, the dome, the water, the wonder. The vision, fully embodied.
- **Who's here:** Samuel + Momo, together. AvenMaia as voice/presence.

## 2. The train — the passage *(between worlds)*

The train Samuel sometimes takes from Schnitzmühle to Munich. The literal bridge between the new world and the old one. A liminal space — reflection, voiceover, watching the green give way to the city.

- **Feeling:** in-between, contemplative. Window light, motion, thought.
- **What we shoot here:** transitions, reflection, the quiet realization beats. Often where AvenMaia and Samuel talk.

## 3. Munich — the legacy home / power-mode desk *(the old world)*

The legacy city homebase. Samuel comes here **2–3 days, weekends only**, to work in pure **power mode** — short, intense bursts at the **outdoor backyard table**. **Momo is not here.** This is the old world he's leaving behind, used now only as a tool: a place to do the focused Burst, then leave.

- **Feeling:** the old world. Concrete, walls, hurry — held at arm's length. Productive but never home.
- **What we shoot here:** the Burst half of the rhythm — the intense focused work session with AvenMaia at the backyard table. The anti-vision ghost we always cut against.
- **Who's here:** Samuel alone (+ AvenMaia). Never Momo.

> The three locations *are* the rhythm: **Munich** = the Burst (work, alone, old world). **The train** = the Handoff (the passage between). **Schnitzmühle** = the Living (presence, Momo, the new world being grown). We cut between them to show the whole life.
